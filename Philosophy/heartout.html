What's that need to said but no one wants to hear?

By Shubham Gautam

Do you think of yourself as a kind person?
Do you believe that you're selflessly good to others?
Then, this goes for you.
I've always thought of myself as a “good person”, a person who'd be always on his toes to help other people. I thought of myself as a kind person, who is generous with others, irrespective of who they are, or from where they belong. I had all these so-called “Good” apparitions of myself until yesterday.
I witnessed an event, which broke off my tinted glasses of “assumptions” leading me through a path of realization. Here it goes:
Yesterday, I was walking with my cousin, Piyush. He's a fun-loving person who would constantly pick you up and get you involved in his “daily adventures”.
We were discussing about some random stuff whilst walking. I wasn't contributing much into the conversation, but I was hearing each word he was offering me.
Just when we were engrossed into this, a car passed us and after traveling about 200mts. of distance ahead us, it stopped abruptly. While we were observing what has happened, a lady nearly in her thirties stepped out followed by her son of about 15, while there was a man seated firmly at the driver's seat, trying to plug on the engine repeatedly. He appeared as if he was the husband, and the only one amongst those three who knew how to drive.
Without wasting much time, the lady and her son started pushing the car, so as to give it a throttle.
While I was observing all this, Piyush called out to me and said, “Let's go”. I was in a dilemma not knowing what to do, but I followed him till the car. He without saying anything, started helping the lady in pushing the car. Then after a while, he asked the lady to move aside and let him do the job.
I was standing there, not knowing what to do. I was just watching what was going on there.
After pushing the car for a while, the engine was ignited again. The man who was handling the stearing, walked out of the car and along with his wife thanked him. While they were about to leave, the lady waved a good bye towards him and said, “God bless you son!”
I didn't moved. I was still standing there.
This incident made me realize many things, which goes as follows:

  • Everyone, literally everyone in this world feels or believes that he/she is a good person, based on what they feel is right. People generally bias their judgements based on selfish “good” deeds.
  • Many of us are good only till places, where we think we'll be benefitted. I've seen people speaking to god after any mis-happening that, “Wasn't I good enough?” or “What did I do to deserve this?”. But do we ask ourselves that “Am I selflessly good?” or “Am I selflessly kind?”. May or may not beacuse no one wants to break off their apprations into realising what they truly are.
  • What do you say after a break-up? “I am a good person, but he/she never cared”. Manier times it happens we feel we're kind to someone, doing good to them. But lemme tell you, many of us are kind to people because we subconsciously or consciously belive that this person in particular has something to offer us. Ask yourself, aren't we selfish?

So, what is something that's need to be said?
That, before you say you're a kind person, always always weigh your statement, introspect yourself so as to know if you're really that kind, or is it just a kindness motivated by reasons. Because, when you say you're kind, you give people hope, you let them expect more from you, you let them believe that you exist only for good and when you fail to match their expectations, they are shattered! Be kind without any reason.
