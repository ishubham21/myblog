Definition of Mature Love

By Shubham Gautam

Sara was sitting close to Avi, close enough to feel each others' breath. They both were silent, as if they were ghosted. There was a sense of unsual serenity all around, only to hear clock going fast with “tik-tik”. Both were calm, superficially.
Sara (without any movement): Tell me.
Avi (puzzeled): Tell you what?
Sara (turning her head towards him, with a lighting gaze in her eyes): Have you stopped loving me?
Avi (looking straight into her eyes): What bothers you?
Sara: Am I not important to you?
Avi (puzzled again): Am I doing less than enough?
Sara (in a low tone): Sometimes, I feel out casted, I feel unwanted. I sometimes question myself if I'm still important to you. There had been days when I felt alone, as if a part in me was missing. And yes, you were missing. You know, I only get to hear from you at night when we both are tired. There had been days too when I felt lonely, and I was helplessly dejected. Tell me, have you stopped loving me?
Avi (in a calm tone): Are you confusing attention with love?
(Sara was puzzled for quite a moment, she was drowned into a medley of good and bad things and morals and what not, she sunk deep into thoughts, thoughts of realization)
Avi: We're adults, and life sometimes can get busy. We've our carrers, our families and our goals which are equally important.
Sara: Do we lack something in our relationship?
Avi: Each others' spaces.
Sara: What if you don't want me anymore?
Avi: I would tell you.
Sara: What if you stop loving me one day?
Avi: I would tell you.
Sara: Why do you give me so many reasons to love you idiot!
Avi: I love you too.
(Sara smiled, seeing her Avi too smiled and he pulled her closer to hug her)

So,the definition of mature love could be:

  • The one where we respect each others' boundaries, where we know when to stop and when to pursue, where you try to understand that your partner can't be available for you throughout the day.
  • The one where honesty prevails. Just like the way Avi was honest with Sara, if everyone would've had resorted to this, our generation could've been a happier one, with a very less number of heart broken people. At times, people think it's cool to hide feelings and play manipulating games. But, lemme tell you that it isn't cool to play guess games with you partner. This would only make them distant from you.
  • Speaking out clearly about the needs and wants. Had Sara not been vocal about what bothered her, they both would've cost their relationship.
  • Trusting and understanding your partners. Trust never means “blind trust”, but trusting your partner for what they have to offer.
  • Self respect. It's always important to set boundaries for yourself. It's important to let yourself and everyone know that you're not just what they want you to be, you're what you're and what you want to. You should be knowing what's good for you and what doesn't work for you.
