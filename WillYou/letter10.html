Letter-10: Do I deserve this?

By Shubham Gautam

Dear Best Friend,

Before writing to you Nazm, I want to ask you one thing! Are you willing to keep it up with me? Are we still friends? Did I make anything bitter of you? Or anyone around me?

This time I'm not addressing you, but to the creator of my sufferings, God.
And for sure you can read it (For You make things bearable).

You biased god, I address this note to you. You did everything wrong to me, I survived, I stood up, I wasn't helped, yet I smiled. You knew that I had only one constant prayer that I wanted to have this child, I wanted my child so bad that I sacrificed everything I build and had and still you took this from me?. Now, you can rest peacefully for I am left with nothing, not even with a hope to survive. Where did I go wrong? Was it no fasting on certain days or not standing up to everything you expected from me?. Why didn't you kill me in the first chance or the second you had? Tell me what was my fault? Tell me What was my child's fault? I am a wailing mother, a mother in pain, who can do nothing. See the irony of my life, I even can't die peacefully, I'm too timid to even end my life. If you can't give me happiness and hope, at least you should've given me courage. You had chances to sacrifice me, but you were adamant to quenched your thirst by my unborn.

I now have millions of reasons to hate you, thank you for making everyday hell here while you can't make me actually die to see the hell, I wish you could. Since you have been so generous in giving me pain, can you give me some answers too, like what to do of the hopes I had for my child, or with the clothes and the toys I had stuffed his room with, or how should I react to the names I had hoped of calling my child. You are biased, you gave me all the sufferings just because I never complained and tolerated everything you put me through. But you know what? I will never bend in front of you! I am stronger than you, I coped! You made Ganesha because your wife couldn't bear the loss of her child, I can't, so I will cry, blame and will eventually live. But let me tell you one thing, I will not give up, I can't see you happy. I will not.

From
An obstinate girl who could have been a mother if you hadn't decided to play on!

I'm sorry Nazm for putting you through all this and not replying to your letters. I was deeply grieved. I was busy giving up and standing up once again. I don't know but you were wrong, I never actually became stronger when I lost everything, instead I grew weaker, anxious and paranoid. But this is a new and stronger me, no attachments and no pain. I will survive, I even after all this still don't have the strength to slit my vessels open, but one day if I do, then this will be the last letter from my side. Thank you Nazm for everything, you are the best thing that has ever happened to me. I sometimes wish I could see you in person to thank you.

With gratitude

Not yet dead
Sara
